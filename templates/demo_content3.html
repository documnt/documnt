Letter from the Editor

The fate of refugees is effectively controlled by the presence or absence of certain documents, Simon Roloff reminds us in his essay for the first issue of Documnt. Timely in bringing experimental literature into contact with politics and aesthetics, it questions the political efficacy of the most prominent techniques in contemporary conceptual literature: "conceptuary writing", algorithmic writing, and "appropriation art".

The other major written work for this issue was written by Bryony Gillard for her MFA thesis at the Dutch Art Institute, an abridged version we publish here. A "constellation" diverging somewhat from traditional essay structures, it is a feminist critique of the male-dominated history of "concrete poetry", exploring the lack of female voices in the established canon, and highlighting a host of feminine and feminist experimental writers and artists who have been ignored.

Documnt is serving as a space for art-theoretical, literary, and political writing and art dealing with technology, language, sound and space. We accept submissions of all kinds (including mixtapes), which can be sent to documntmag@gmail.com, which we publish and make available for download on our website:

documnt.net

We are constantly working on a political/aesthetic reading/viewing/handling of documents, from receipts to band-aids... Yes, Documnt aims at manipulating this Internet tangle of data "debris" in hopes of discovering new relationships between analog and digital, theory and practice. We welcome anyone interested.
